Overcrowded Schools in Sunset Park

Sunset Park, Brooklyn, has some of the most overcrowded schools in New York.
This issue has been wildly covered by the media, but still affects thousands of children in the community.

Over 2,600 students are affected by overcrowding in schools in Sunset Park. Over 9 elementary schools in the area are beyond capacity. Teachers claim that they lose personal connections with students. Students feel like not enough attention is brought to them

Many of the children in the community are forced to take buses to schools in South Slope because there is no space in their local schools. This makes it harder for parents to commute to pick up their children or have a conversation with the teachers.

Over 51% of the population in Sunset Park is foreign born. The children who are most affected by the overcrowding in schools are those of immigrant parents and those from low income households.

Because the schools are at maximum capacity, a lot of the resources are scarce. Many children cannot participate in gym class, or take art and music. School lunches have to be served as early as 10:30 to be able to cover all of the students.

The city has designated funding for 6 new schools that will supposedly be built and functioning by 2021. Some believe that it will not be enough, while others argue that parents should be involved in the planning process so that kids can have the best education possible.
